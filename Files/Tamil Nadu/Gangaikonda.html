Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple

The Brihadisvara Temple, also known as Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva located in Gangaikonda Cholapuram, Ariyalur district, Tamil Nadu, India. It was built by Rajendra Chola I in the 11th century CE as part of his new capital. The temple is an example of Chola architecture and is smaller yet more refined than the Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur. It is part of the Great Living Chola Temples and was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2004. The temple is an active site with four daily rituals and several yearly festivals, including Maha Sivaratri and Sadhaya vila.
